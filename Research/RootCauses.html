This explains how the Fundamental Therom of Algebra (all polynomials P(z):=a1zn+a2zn-1+...+an-1z+an has a zero in C [the complex numbers]) expands to quaternions and octonions.

Because quaternions and octonions aren't communitive (ABâ‰ BA), polynomials for octonions are defined as P(o):=a0oa1o...ano+(lower terms), and the am constants are quaternions.

The therom is proved with lemmas (proofs used in a proof of a larger therom) showing these polynomials are continuous and homotopic (there a continuous function between any polynomials), and another lemma that I dont understand (Lemma 4: "The map has degree pn in the sense of Brouwer.").

The proof uses contridition to assume that a polynomial maps from K to K\{0} (has a domain in the algebra over the octonions and has a range in the algebra without the point "0") and shows how this forms a contridiction with Lemma 4.
